Team Information

Our Caracal Robotics team, which consists of special education class, preparatory class and mainly 9th and 10th grade students, allows the participation of every student who adopts our mission and vision.

While our social team is responsible for areas such as sponsorship, social responsibility, team advertising, our technical team plays a role in areas such as robot design, software, mechanics, etc.

We take care to ensure the balance of girls and boys in the distribution of tasks of our Social and Technical teams and to distribute equal tasks. Our team consists of 36 students, 5 teacher mentors, a mentor from the professional sector and 3 university student mentors.

Our mission, as Turkey’s first software high school among the changing and developing vocational high schools, is to provide our students with experience by participating in various competitions and events in the field of software and engineering and help them to become entrepreneurial individuals who know how to be together, work, be virtuous, respectful and tolerant.

The Robot Building Process

During the construction of the robot, there were many ideas floating around. These ideas converged and evolved over time. As we encountered mistakes, we corrected them, leading to the creation of this robot. Initially, the robot's concept involved an articulated forearm design that could move up and down, a concept used by other teams as well. However, even though we transitioned to a fixed angle later on, our mission was to adapt to every alliance's needs, and we couldn't achieve this with the fixed angle. To address this issue, we adopted the system used by the Everybot to score in the amphitheater, modifying it into a telescopic arm to prevent it from getting caught on chains.

In-game Strategies

We adapt our in-game role according to our team's capabilities. We designed our robot to harmonize with other alliance robots. Let me explain our compatibility with other robots through a few combinations.

Defense

If our alliance has an attacking robot capable of scoring and assaulting, our robot can both relay notes from the human player and engage in defense.

Offense

If our alliance comprises a defensive robot and one capable of scoring in the amphitheater, our robot, equipped with an intake system that can only pick up notes from the ground and score through the speaker, contributes points for our alliance.

Amphitheater

If our alliance includes a robot designated for scoring through the speaker and another for defense, our robot solely focuses on gathering notes from the source and depositing them in the amphitheater, thereby increasing the speaker's points and facilitating amphitheater scoring.

PEY PROJECT

Pey, short for "The Way of Potanciel Pey Equalency" is a transformative initiative aimed at fostering equal opportunities in Şırnak, a small province in Turkey. Over the course of 10 weeks, we conducted classes covering various subjects such as C#, Tinkercad, and Scratch. We even had the privilege of sponsoring two exceptional students to represent Şırnak in the Marmara Regional competition in Istanbul, covering all their expenses through generous sponsorships.

The school we collaborated with faces significant challenges due to poor infrastructure, yet they are unwavering in their commitment to provide quality education. In a testament to their dedication, they repurposed a teacher's room into a fully functional classroom. Despite grappling with internet connectivity issues and other adversities, we remained steadfast in our mission. We firmly believe that where there's a will to learn, there's a way to overcome obstacles.

Furthermore, recognizing the importance of providing access to resources, we established a laboratory equipped with 10 computers in Şırnak. This initiative not only facilitates the implementation of our project but also empowers the local community to pursue their interests and further their own development.

Two years ago, there was a very strong earthquake in this region and millions of people were affected by this earthquake, especially children could not continue their normal education in schools. We are introducing FIRST and STEM culture in Hatay, which is one of the cities affected by this earthquake and in the recovery process, and we will open an FLL team here next year.

CRC

The aim of this project is to instill a STEM culture in students from a young age, increase interest in engineering, and teach FIRST values. Various activities are being carried out in collaboration with TEV Celalettin Buluğ Primary School in our district. Houge Dreams With Small Hands

In the "Houge Dreams With Small Hands" project, we divided our team into two groups: the teachers and the hands-on activity hosts. The teachers, comprising the first group, provided explanations about our purpose, our activities, and our identity. As Caracal Robotics, we also developed an interactive storybook to enhance the learning experience. We aimed to serve as role models for these students, allowing them to share in our experiences.

Meanwhile, the hands-on activity hosts, forming the second group, engaged the students in various activities such as creating photo frames and coloring robots. Our intention was not merely to impart knowledge and leave but to establish a familial bond with the students, fostering a warm and welcoming environment for all.

Caracal Goodness On The Way Project

As 9545 Caracal Robotics, we are conscious and aware youths aiming to draw attention to an endangered species, as implied by our team name. Since the inception of our team, we have continuously sought answers to what we can do for humanity or the world.

As a team valuing the importance of healing our wounds together and unity within society, we highly regard civil society organizations. For instance, through collaborative efforts with prominent civil society organizations like LÖSEV, we aim to raise and instill awareness, particularly regarding leukemia, and contribute to the fight against cancer.
